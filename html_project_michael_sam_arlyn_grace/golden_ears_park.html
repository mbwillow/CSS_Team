About Golden Ears Park

Golden Ears Provincial Park is a Provincial Park in British Columbia, Canada at 555.9 km². It is named after the prominent twin peaks which are commonly referred to as Golden Ears (Mount Blanshard) (Elevation: 1716 m or 5630 ft). The park's southern end is located on the northern edge of the district municipality of Maple Ridge on the north side of the Fraser River.

Sam's Review

Since my family lives in Maple Ridge, we often go to Golden Ears park for some relaxing outdoor recreation. There are countless activities to enjoy in the park. Whether you have a family or not, are an avid outdoor person or not, there is something for you at Golden Ears park.

If you are looking for a short adventure, you can stop at the Spirea trail (only takes 20 minutes to walk the loop trail). Spirea trail is a beautiful loop around a bog and has informational boards along the way providing information about the wildlife and habitat. After Spirea trail, make your way to Alouette lake to enjoy a picnic on one of park tables or bring a canoe or kayak and set out on the lake. There is plenty of space for lawn games and there are additional trails to walk near the lake.

If you are really feeling adventurous and you have good hiking gear, then I highly recommend tackling the Golden Ears trail. It is an approximately 12 km (one-way) trail departing from the West Canyon parking lot. Suggested time is 7 hours, but can definitely take longer. I hiked to the summit with a group of friends and our round trip was about 15 hours, though we were a bit slow going at times. I have heard of others taking about 7-10 hours if you are really booking it.

Attractions

Here are some highlighted attractions the park has to offer. You can find more info activity suggestions on the Golden Ears Park website.

Hiking

Golden Ears has some of the best hikes and walks in the greater Vancouver area. From short 20 minute walks to overnight hikes, there is a trail for everyone to enjoy. Be sure to pack water and some food for any of the hikes other than the Spirea trail, which is a short walk.

Some of my favorite walks/hikes
Hike/Walk Notes
Spirea trail Easy walking trail with information boards along the loop trail.
Lower Falls trail Easy, mostly flat walking trail to lower water falls. Should take about 1 hour for 2.7 km.
Viewpoint trail Easy to moderate hike to a nice picturesque sandy beach by a creek. It is 1.5 km one way and takes about 45 minutes each way. There is an elevation gain of 175m. In the rainy season there small waterfalls along the trail.
Golden Ears trail Difficult and steep elevation gains. This is not for beginners! It is 12 km one way and has an elevation change of 1500 meteres. Be sure to pack and dress for strenous activity.

Camping

Drive-in camping is available, but you will have to make reservations during the summer as it fills up quickly.
